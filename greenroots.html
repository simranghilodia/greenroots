Project Proposal: GreenRoots – Empowering Organic Farming

1. Introduction

In recent years, consumers have become increasingly aware of the health and environmental benefits of organic produce. However, despite rising demand, India's organic farming ecosystem faces significant hurdles, including limited farmer adoption, lack of guidance, and unfair pricing due to middlemen.

GreenRoots proposes to create a digital platform that not only educates and empowers farmers to transition to organic practices, but also connects them directly to end consumers through a transparent, fair-priced marketplace.

2. Problem Statement

  • Limited adoption of organic farming: Many farmers lack access to proper training, resources, or incentives to switch to organic methods.
  • Middlemen exploitation: Farmers who do grow organic produce often have to sell through intermediaries, earning very little despite charging a premium to consumers.
  • Consumer distrust: Buyers are often unsure if produce labeled “organic” is genuinely certified or worth the premium price.
  • Lack of direct farmer-to-consumer platforms: Few reliable systems connect organic farmers directly with consumers, limiting income and trust on both ends.

This system discourages sustainable farming and deprives both farmers and consumers of the full benefits of organic produce.

3. Proposed Solution

GreenRoots will be a digital platform designed to solve this dual challenge:

  • Helping farmers transition to organic farming through step-by-step guidance and certification support.
  • Providing a direct-to-consumer marketplace where verified organic farmers can sell their products at transparent, fair prices.

The goal is to build a trust-based ecosystem that promotes health, sustainability, and farmer prosperity.

4. Key Features

a) Farmer Onboarding & Training Portal

  • Modules on how to convert to organic farming.
  • Video tutorials, expert tips, and multilingual content.
  • Support for certification (e.g., PGS or NPOP in India).

b) Verified Organic Certification

  • Guidance on obtaining certification.
  • On-platform verification badge for certified farmers.
  • Upload and track certification progress.

c) Online Organic Marketplace

  • Farmers list their products directly (vegetables, grains, spices, etc.).
  • Transparent pricing with no middlemen.
  • Product traceability and origin info for consumers.

d) Consumer Side Features

  • Search by product or farmer.
  • Location-based delivery filtering.
  • Customer reviews, freshness ratings, and delivery scheduling.

e) Price Fairness & Revenue Sharing

  • Platform sets benchmark prices by region and crop.
  • Farmers retain 85–90% of the sale value.
  • Buyers get affordable, verified organic goods.

5. Target Audience

  • Farmers: Small and mid-sized landholders interested in organic farming.
  • Consumers: Health-conscious urban buyers seeking genuine organic produce.
  • Institutions: Schools, hospitals, cafes interested in sustainable sourcing.
  • NGOs & Govts: Partner organizations looking to support sustainable agriculture.

6. Future Scope

  • Launch in 1–2 pilot states (e.g., Gujarat, Maharashtra, or Uttarakhand).
  • Mobile app for rural farmer access with offline content features.
  • Cold chain logistics partnerships for perishables.
  • Integration with government schemes for subsidies and incentives.
  • Export facilitation for surplus certified organic produce.

7. Impact and Goals

  • Support at least 10,000 farmers in transitioning to certified organic practices within 2 years.
  • Create a sustainable and transparent supply chain from soil to shelf.
  • Eliminate middlemen exploitation by empowering farmers to price and sell directly.
  • Build consumer trust in organic food with full traceability and certification visibility.
